HEARTFELT ARTS - Empowering Women Artisans through Technology, Skills and Community

Heartfelt Arts empowers women artisans to leverage their newly learned skills to create beautiful handicrafts and products which generate a sustainable income. Women graduates from AMMACHI Labs’ unique computerized training are presented with opportunities and community support to market and sell their arts through tight-kn


To date there have been 13 trade shows run by the over 461 graduates from the 36 Cooperatives in Tamil Nadu, Kerala and other parts of India. Nine new small businesses were created by graduates. Heartfelt Arts also showcases the beautiful handicrafts on a stunning online catalog for the whole world to browse. Sustainable livelihood and creative expression through arts have encouraged these women to break through traditional barriers, cultural taboos and limitations. Many artisans have gained the confidence to market and sell their products regionally, nationally and even internationally.

The societal and economic impact of Heartfelt Arts has aided in alleviating poverty while giving the women the self-confidence to generate prosperity for their families and communities.

Women Empowerment Project

The Women Empowerment Project has trained over 4000 women in South India through Amrita University’s unique computerized vocational education training and life enrichment programs by AMMACHI Labs.

“The United Nations scours the world looking for the best projects, the most innovative projects, the projects which will have the highest impact,” said Ms. Grande. “The one we’ve chosen for India is this project, and we chose it because AMMACHI Labs is the most creative way of empowering women. Of all of the projects which the UN funds around the world, this is the one dearest to our heart. It’s the one we are most proud of. And it’s the one that we intend to take to the rest of the world so that they can learn from what you have achieved.”

Ms Lise Grande, Head of United Nations, India
