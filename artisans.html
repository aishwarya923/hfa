Every woman has a unique story of her own. Here are a few profiles of the women artisans.

Sreelekha

Sreelekha learned about the free Artificial Jewelry Making”” course offered through the Women Empowerment Project when, in her own words, she had reached the lowest point in her life.

“As soon as I learned that I was accepted into this course, I felt like things were changing for the better,” she explains. “The first day of the course filled me with such joy and confidence that I felt as though I could overcome any hardship; this would be my new beginning. The course helped reinstate my self-confidence and rebuild my life. The money I earned from my first sale gave me the confidence that I could earn money this way.”

Sreelekha

Absa

Absa is a talented bag maker, but it wasn’t until she attended the Women Empowerment Project’s Fabric Painting course that Absa recognized her abilities and gained confidence to earn money from her craft. Absa reports has begun to earn well making and selling her own bags since taking the course, an achievement she says was only possible after developing confidence in herself and her abilities, through participating in the WE Project’s course.

“I had completed the 12th grade. Even so, I was never able to speak openly, if had a problem. Now, after the painting class, I am able to speak up. Before, even I had to go to Adimali [15 km from home], I would have to take somebody with me. But now, I don’t need that. That is because of this course. I say this with 100 percent confidence: I can go anywhere, if I have an address – I can find it. Confidence is the biggest thing I got from this course.”

Absa

Mini KM

Mini realized her dream of opening up her very own business. The name of her shop is Amrita Fancine and is located in the heart of Mananthavady Town in Kerala, and offers a wide range of original creations – painted fabric and artificial jewelry products. Since opening her shop, Mini has taken steps to further develop her skills and talents in traditional techniques such as Kalamkari and Temple Mural Painting.

“My life has totally changed. Through this course, I was able to understand the skills within me. My self-confidence has increased and I took a strong decision to make this as a source of income. Now I am running a shop. I would like to expand my business by doing fabric painting works and to teach others about this course. I am satisfied that I am able to make a living and also feel very happy to share what I have learned with others. Now I’m planning to enroll many students and through that expand my business. Also, I will be sure to find time to learn new things!”

Mini KM

Kalavati

Kalavati, the eldest student in her batch, was one of the first to sign up when she learned the Artificial Jewelry Making course was being offered in her village. Since graduating from the course, Kalavati remains active as an entrepreneur and student leader.

“I was worried about having to ask my husband and children for money. But now, I need not depend on anybody for money. I am able to earn and spend for what I need freely. I learned that I have to make innovative models, according to the demand of the buyers. I would boast to everyone that I was learning through computers. Being exposed to computers at this stage in my life brings me such happiness.”

Kalavati

Sharmila

Sharmila co-founded Amrita Fabrics, a group business with fellow graduates from the Fabric Painting course offered through the Women Empowerment Project. Since the course Sharmila and the Amrita Fabrics team have upgraded their painting skills and have become artisans of fabric painted mural temple art. Sharmila is also the President of her Cooperative group.

"Our self-confidence has increased every day. The fact that I could do that made me so happy, I can’t even express. I’ve completed trainings before this course, but never experienced anything like this before. Most of us don’t get the chance to recognize our hidden talents."

Sharmila
